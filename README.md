# Olympic Sponsorship ROI & Brand Exposure Analysis

##  Overview
This project analyzes sponsorship performance and brand exposure during the Olympic Games. The goal is to evaluate how different sponsorship strategies impact audience engagement, brand visibility, and return on investment (ROI).

---

##  Problem Statement
Companies invest millions in Olympic sponsorships, but measuring the effectiveness of these investments can be challenging. This project aims to provide a data-driven approach to understanding which factors contribute most to successful sponsorship outcomes.

---

##  Objectives
- Analyze sponsorship ROI across different brands
- Evaluate audience engagement levels
- Identify trends in advertising performance
- Understand how demographics influence engagement

---

##  Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## Methodology

- Cleaned and validated dataset (60 records, no missing values)  
- Aggregated sponsor performance by impressions and brand lift  
- Analyzed platform-level exposure and engagement performance  
- Evaluated sport-level sponsorship visibility  
- Conducted ROI analysis by comparing advertising spend and brand lift  
- Visualized trends using bar charts and scatter plots

---

##  Key Analysis Performed
- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- ROI and engagement trend visualization
- Comparative analysis across platforms and audiences

---

## Key Insights

- Coca-Cola, Visa, and Samsung generated the highest total sponsor impressions  
- TikTok and YouTube produced the strongest engagement and exposure  
- Figure Skating and Alpine Skiing delivered the highest sponsorship visibility  
- Average engagement rate ≈ 6.63% and average brand lift ≈ 10.99%  
- Higher ad spend generally correlates with increased brand lift, though not perfectly linear
